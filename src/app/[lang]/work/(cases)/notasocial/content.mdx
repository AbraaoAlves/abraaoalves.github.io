# NotaSocial — Founder & Full-Stack Engineer

**Fortaleza, Brazil · 2014 – 2016**

Before I spent nine years deep in one platform, I built one from nothing. NotaSocial was a
mobile-first donation platform that turned an everyday Brazilian habit — asking for a *nota
fiscal* (electronic invoice) at the checkout — into a frictionless way to fund charities. I
founded it and owned the entire stack: Ionic on the phone, Node.js and MongoDB on the
server, OCR in between, and no dedicated PM or designer to hide behind.

## The idea: receipts that give

In Brazil, consumers can assign their electronic invoices to a registered charity, turning
everyday spending into tax-deductible donations. The mechanism existed; the experience
didn't. Using it meant hoarding paper receipts and typing long codes into a clunky
government portal — so almost nobody did. NotaSocial collapsed that into three taps: snap
the receipt, pick a cause, done.

## Building it solo

The interesting engineering lived in the seam between a photo and a donation. The app
scanned a receipt, OCR'd the 44-digit invoice access key, validated it, and registered the
donation against the chosen charity.

```ts
// The 44-digit access key is the spine of a Brazilian e-invoice. OCR is
// noisy, so we validated structure + check digit before trusting a scan.
function parseAccessKey(raw: string): string | null {
  const digits = raw.replace(/\D/g, "");
  if (digits.length !== 44) return null;
  return isValidCheckDigit(digits) ? digits : null;
}
```

Owning the whole stack alone forces a kind of honesty: every shortcut is one you'll
maintain, and every abstraction has to earn its keep — there's no one else to explain it to.

## Validation

NotaSocial took **1st place at the III Startup Weekend Fortaleza** and was one of **12 global
finalists at the Global Startup Battle 2014**, out of thousands of teams worldwide. Not bad
for a receipt scanner.

## What zero-to-one taught me

Founding NotaSocial is why I now read architecture decisions the way I do — not as
abstractions, but as bets someone has to live with. The instinct to build software that
lasts didn't come from a big company. It came from being the person who answered the pager
at 2am for something I'd shipped myself.
